Welcome to Cambridge

The University of Cambridge is a global institution of excellence in education, research, and innovation. Founded in 1209, it has shaped the minds of some of the greatest thinkers in history.

History

Cambridge was founded by scholars leaving Oxford after a dispute. Over the centuries, it has become a beacon of academic brilliance. The university has produced over 100 Nobel laureates and has been home to legendary figures like Isaac Newton, Alan Turing, and Rosalind Franklin.

Academics

Cambridge offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate degrees across six schools: Arts and Humanities, Biological Sciences, Clinical Medicine, Humanities and Social Sciences, Physical Sciences, and Technology. It is known for its rigorous tutorial system and world-class faculty.

Campus Life

With 31 autonomous colleges, Cambridge provides a unique blend of academic and social life. Students enjoy rowing on the River Cam, participating in over 700 clubs and societies, and exploring the historic architecture and green spaces of the city.
